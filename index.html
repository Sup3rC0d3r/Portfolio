Learning to Code

"Handcoding my first porfolio site has sharpened my web development skills. I'm bursting with knowledge and insight that was once was shrouded in mystery. I'm delighted because as I continue to code I'm witnessing my creation come to life, and take on a life of its own. I'm delighted, as if a parent to a child, seeing the world again, anew. And as my child grows, changes, discovers new realities, so do I. It's wonderful.

Granted. I have a strong desire to do excellent work. Before I wrote my first line of code for my porfolio site, I took every lesson in Codecademy's "CodeYear challenge". At times it was challenging, but at the end of it, I didn't feel ready to begin real web development. How would I take the knowledge I gained from their in-the-browswer code editor and translate it into a real project. I wasn't sure. So I started "googling".

I quickly discovered tuts+ Which was a goldmine for me. Specificallly, courses taught by Jeffrey Way. He's a great teacher, that really allows you to see behind-the-curtain as he walks you to new insights.
